There are a surprising number of myths and legends surrounding the history of the University at Albany, but the M.E. Grenander Department of Special Collections and Archives is here to set the record straight on what is fact and what is fiction!.

FACT

These facts are amazingly true!

Students once routinely moved the Minerva statue as a campus prank. It is hollow and surprisingly light! Fullscreen
The University started using “University at Albany, SUNY” in Fall 1986, but the SUNY Board of Trustees never formally approved the change, so the official name is still the “State University of New York at Albany.”
“Podiating” is the formal term for laying down somewhere on the podium. Fullscreen
The Campus Center was previously home to a barber shop, pub, and even a 12-lane bowling alley! Media
When “SUNYA” was a common name for the school in the 1970s, it was typically pronounced “SOON-yuh” not “SOON-ee-ay.” Media
The podium was the second largest poured-in-place concrete structure in the world when it was constructed, second only to the Pentagon. Media
The basement of the Dewey Library was once used as a basketball court, pillars and all! Media

Parking

Visitor Lot 2 is an unattended parking lot. Once inside the Visitor Lot, credit cards are accepted. Visitor parking is $5.00 (US) for the day.

After exiting your car, go to the opposite end of the Visitor Parking Lot from the entrance, turn right and walk along Indigenous Drive toward the Science Library. There will be a dorm on your left side that you will pass. The Science Library will be the white building directly in front of you and to the left. Enter two sets of doors and turn left to enter the atrium area. Proceed fully into the atrium to the elevators in the back. Take an elevator to the third level, turn right when you exit the elevator and then turn right again in the hallway. The entrance to Special Collections will be directly ahead of you.
